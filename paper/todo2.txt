● ★ Insight ─────────────────────────────────────
  The scientific-critical-thinking skill's claim-evaluation heuristic applies
  here: before costing a revision, determine whether the revision actually
  addresses the reviewer's underlying concern, or merely trades one limitation
  for a different one at the same level of generality. Reviewers M3 and M4
  question scope, not architecture specifically — so a narrower, cheaper
  intervention may resolve them equally well.
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Cost-Benefit Analysis: Architecture + Task Swap

  1. Implementation Cost — Moderate-to-High

  Code changes themselves are small; experimental-design changes are not.

  Mechanical swap (low cost, ~1–2 days):
  - torchvision.models.efficientnet_b0(weights=EfficientNet_B0_Weights.IMAGENET1
  K_V1) + replace classifier head. ~20 LOC in src/util/cnn.py.
  - Adjust input resize to 224×224 (affects the preprocessing pipeline's
  position in the chain — your transforms currently operate on your own input
  size).
  - Update src/util/gradcam.py target layer (EfficientNet's final conv block,
  not your 4-block's).

  Design decisions (high cost, ~1–2 weeks):
  - Transfer learning regime: fine-tune end-to-end, freeze backbone, or
  progressive unfreezing? Each is a different experiment with different
  dynamics. Reviewer would ask why you chose one.
  - Task redefinition (melanoma vs. benign): HAM10000's seven classes must be
  remapped — mel (n≈1113) vs. nevi nv (n≈6705) is the standard dichotomy,
  creating a ~6:1 imbalance. This invalidates your current dataset composition:
    - The "Healthy Skin" dataset becomes irrelevant and must be dropped.
    - Class imbalance requires stratified sampling, class weights, or focal loss
   — each a new methodological commitment.
    - Your grid-search parameters were optimized on the current task; they may
  not transfer.
  - Re-run grid search on new task to re-justify preprocessing parameters (m8 in
   the review already flags this concern).

  2. Compute Cost — 2–4× current budget

  Against your planned 650-run campaign (5 seeds × 130 configs):

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────┬───────────────────────────┐
  │                 Factor                  │        Multiplier         │
  ├─────────────────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────┤
  │ EfficientNet-B0 vs. custom 4-block      │ ~2.5–3× per forward pass  │
  ├─────────────────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────┤
  │ Larger input (224² vs. current)         │ ~1.5× (if current < 224²) │
  ├─────────────────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────┤
  │ Pretrained weights → faster convergence │ ~0.6× fewer epochs        │
  ├─────────────────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────┤
  │ Net per-run cost                        │ ~2–3×                     │
  ├─────────────────────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────┤
  │ New grid search to establish parameters │ +1 full search            │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────┴───────────────────────────┘

  On the RTX 4080 SUPER this remains feasible but pushes the budget from
  tractable-overnight to multi-day, particularly if you preserve the custom CNN
  for comparison (see §4).

  3. Writing Cost — Effectively a rewrite

  This is the hidden cost that dominates the decision.

  - Title / Abstract / Intro: must rescope — the current framing ("skin disease
  classification") is replaced by a narrower, different claim.
  - Methods §III: architecture section fully rewritten; dataset section fully
  rewritten; loss + class-balance handling new; grid-search justification new.
  - Results §IV: every table and figure regenerates. Table II (all 65
  configurations), Figs. 1–4 — all invalidated.
  - Discussion: your central empirical claim — the E → {D,CS} → N template — may
   not replicate on EfficientNet. The ordering-vs-selection decomposition could
  shift qualitatively. If it does, your contribution reframes entirely; if it
  doesn't, that negative result itself becomes the new headline.
  - Grad-CAM analysis: re-run on a new architecture with new class semantics.
  The single-image criticism (m11, Q4) persists regardless.

  Estimate: 60–80% of the manuscript rewritten, not revised.

  4. Research Relevance — Partial; not proportionate to cost

  Here the critical-thinking framework is unforgiving. Restate the reviewer
  concerns literally:

  - M3 asks for scoped claims, not a different architecture. The remedy the
  reviewer explicitly suggests is "the abstract and conclusion should be more
  carefully scoped." Swapping to EfficientNet replaces one single-architecture
  study with a different single-architecture study — the external validity 
  problem is structurally identical. You would still be unable to claim transfer
   to ResNet, ViT, etc.
  - M4 asks for transparency about class balance, test-set size, and the ceiling
   effect. Switching to melanoma vs. benign does yield a harder, clinically
  standard task — this is the one genuine gain. But the reviewer asks you to
  discuss the ceiling, not eliminate it.
  - M1 (single-seed) is already being addressed by your 5-seed infrastructure —
  orthogonal to this decision.

  The swap resolves ~30–40% of the substantive criticism at ~200–400% of the
  remaining revision cost.

  A Dominated Alternative

  A dramatically more efficient response to M3 is a partial cross-architecture 
  replication:

  1. Keep the existing 650-run campaign on the custom CNN as the primary
  experiment — it is statistically rigorous once multi-seed lands.
  2. Add EfficientNet-B0 only on length-3 pipelines (24 configs × 5 seeds = 120
  runs, ~20% additional compute).
  3. In Results §IV-F, present a single sub-section: "Cross-architecture
  replication of the ordering-dominance finding."
  4. In Discussion, this becomes evidence for generalization of the central
  claim, not against it.

  This addresses M3 directly with a stronger argument (the finding replicates on
   a different architecture) than the swap would provide (the finding exists on 
  a different architecture), at ~5× lower total cost and zero manuscript 
  rewriting — only an additional subsection.

  For M4, a separate low-cost intervention: re-run the existing pipelines on
  melanoma-vs-benign as a supplementary experiment (one table, ~130 runs, no new
   methodology), letting you report both the current easier task (where variance
   decomposition is statistically clean) and the harder clinical task (where
  generalization is addressed).

  Recommendation

  Do not swap. The cost-benefit is adverse and the intervention does not
  structurally resolve the cited concerns. Instead:

  1. Keep the custom CNN as primary.
  2. Add EfficientNet length-3 replication as cross-architecture check (~120
  runs).
  3. Add melanoma-vs-benign on the existing architecture as clinical-relevance
  check (~130 runs).
  4. In revision response, cite both as direct responses to M3 and M4.

  This preserves the novel contribution (the ordering-dominance finding),
  increases its evidentiary weight, and addresses the scope concerns at a
  fraction of the cost of the full swap.

  major comment as a demand to redo the entire experiment rather than as a
  request for scoping, evidence, or transparency. Reading M3 and M4 literally —
  they ask for discussion and additional context, not architectural substitution
   — typically reveals a much cheaper path that is also more rhetorically honest
   in the response letter.
